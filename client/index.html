Ben Israeli offers a 360° tutoring solution to enhance quantitative and language skills for great test prep results and year-round academic success.


Ben provides high school math (algebra, geometry, precalculus, calculus, statistics), biology (SAT II and AP), chemistry (SAT II and AP), Spanish (SAT II), and SAT, ACT, and SAT II Subject Tests tutoring. He can also teach Exeter Math. Every session is one-to-one with Ben personally. Ben fully customizes his teaching style to his student’s learning style. Ben only takes on a limited number of students per year and families make a commitment to working with Ben at his office, at home, or online. Students will receive various licenses to learning websites, which Ben uses to manage student-progress outside of session.

Mr. Israeli approaches education from many angles, structuring sessions to advance the student on a learning track tailored to his/her learning needs. Key to Ben's teaching philosophy is soliciting the student’s active, consistent engagement in the learning process. The goal is each student’s management of his/her learning.

“What is extremely gratifying to me is organizing content for my students that accelerates their learning. Taking into account each student’s unique profile helps determine which exercises will make our sessions most dynamic and impactful, while allowing us to maximize the probability of a successful learning outcome. When students engage in a way that helps them understand what learning can feel like, then my job is done.”

Mr. Israeli offers regular session hours, as well as weekend and online appointments.

Ben Israeli graduated from New York University in 2009 with a degree in Economics and English & American Literature. He has been a full-time tutor since 2010.

Although the ACT covers a wider range of topics than the SAT, it places greater demands on a student's ability to speed-read and move quickly through the test material. An added benefit of taking the ACT is that many schools - even some Ivies- accept it without the two SAT II subjects that are normally required for students taking the SAT. Ben generally recommends the ACT for students who feel they can work quickly.

The redesigned SAT gives students the option of taking the writing section. The math section draws from a narrower range of topics while placing greater emphasis on the student's ability to read, parse, and decipher problems more complex than those from the former SAT. Compared to the ACT, the SAT requires stronger critical thinking skills. Ben generally recommends the SAT for students who have strong conceptual and problem solving skills but who may not work quickly enough to shine on the ACT.

The SAT II biology exam is a wonderful challenge for ninth and tenth grade students looking to establish the first important piece of their college applications. Getting this test out of the way early gives students more time to prepare for the ACT and SAT come crunch-time Junior year. Ben normally recommends this test for students taking advanced placement or intensive biology, depending on what the school offers.

The SAT II math exam is a great way to prove to colleges that you are ready for college level mathematics coursework. While the test stops short of calculus, it covers a wide range algebra, geometry, trigonometry, and pre-calculus topics. Students are given 60 minutes to answer 50 challenging questions. Those who are up for the challenge will be rewarded as this test has a very forgiving curve. Answering as few as 43 out of the 50 questions can still result in a score of 800!
